<p><ul><li>ResistanceSensor</li><li>ThermocoupleSensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The duct temperature sensors market features primarily two types: resistance sensors and thermocouple sensors. Resistance sensors, such as RTDs and thermistors, measure temperature changes through variations in electrical resistance and are known for their accuracy and stability. In contrast, thermocouple sensors consist of two dissimilar metals joined together, generating a voltage proportional to temperature differences, making them suitable for high-temperature applications. Both types are essential for HVAC systems, enhancing energy efficiency and maintaining desired environmental conditions within ductwork.</p></p>
